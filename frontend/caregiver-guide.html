Understanding Disease Stages

Early Stage

🌱 Mild / Early

Memory lapses, difficulty with complex tasks, personality shifts. Person can still live independently with some support.

Middle Stage

🌿 Moderate

Increased memory loss, confusion with time/place, needs help with daily activities. Supervision is important.

Late Stage

🍂 Severe

Loss of ability to communicate verbally. Full-time care required. Focus shifts to comfort and quality of life.

Watch For

⚠️ Warning Signs

Sudden confusion, aggression, wandering at night, refusal to eat, or unexplained falls warrant immediate medical attention.

Medication Management

💊 Best Practices

  • Use a weekly pill organiser with AM/PM compartments
  • Give medications at the same time daily — link to meals or routines
  • Never crush or split tablets without pharmacist approval
  • Keep an updated medication list with doses and timings
  • Contact the doctor before stopping or adjusting any medication

🌿 Tip: Handling Refusal

If the patient refuses medication, stay calm. Try offering it with a preferred drink or food, or wait 10–15 minutes and try again. Never force medication. If refusal is consistent, speak with the doctor about alternative formulations (liquid, patch).

⚠️ Watch for Side Effects

Common medications like donepezil, memantine, or antipsychotics can cause nausea, dizziness, or increased confusion. Report any sudden behaviour changes, falls, or unusual drowsiness to the treating physician immediately.

Home Safety Checklist

🔒 Prevent Wandering

  • Install door alarms or motion sensors at exits
  • Place a STOP sign at exit doors at eye level
  • Ensure patient wears an ID bracelet at all times
  • Use location sharing on this platform regularly

🍳 Kitchen Safety

  • Install stove knob covers or auto-shutoff devices
  • Lock away cleaning products and sharp objects
  • Set water heater below 48°C (120°F) to prevent scalding
  • Remove or label expired food regularly

🚿 Bathroom Safety

  • Add non-slip mats in shower and near toilet
  • Install grab bars beside toilet and shower
  • Consider a shower chair for stability
  • Keep the bathroom well-lit at all times

💡 General Home

  • Remove loose rugs and clutter from walkways
  • Install nightlights in hallways and bathrooms
  • Label rooms and drawers with pictures + text
  • Keep the environment calm and familiar

Communication Tips

🗣️ How to Communicate Effectively

  • Speak slowly and clearly using simple, short sentences
  • Use their name and maintain gentle eye contact
  • Ask one question at a time — offer yes/no options when possible
  • Never argue or correct — redirect instead
  • Use a calm, warm tone even when frustrated
  • Non-verbal cues (touch, smile, gesture) matter greatly in late stages

🌿 Tip: When They Repeat Themselves

Repetition is a symptom, not stubbornness. Respond to the emotion behind the repeated question, not just the question itself. If they ask "When is dinner?" repeatedly, they may really be expressing hunger or anxiety — address that feeling directly.

Emergency Contacts & Resources

⚠️ Call Emergency Services Immediately If:

  • Patient has fallen and is unresponsive or in pain
  • Signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Patient has wandered and cannot be located
  • Severe agitation or aggressive behaviour with risk of harm

Caregiver Self-Care

🧘 You Matter Too

Caregiver burnout is real. Chronic stress, sleep deprivation, and social isolation are common. You cannot give quality care if you are depleted. Self-care is not selfish — it is essential.

😌 Signs of Burnout

  • Constant exhaustion even after rest
  • Feeling resentful, hopeless, or irritable
  • Neglecting your own health needs
  • Withdrawing from friends and family

🌱 Recovery Steps

  • Ask for and accept help from family members
  • Use respite care services for regular breaks
  • Join a caregiver support group
  • Speak to a counsellor or therapist

🍃 Daily Micro-Breaks

Even 10 minutes of intentional rest makes a difference. Step outside, do breathing exercises, listen to music, or simply sit in silence. Schedule these breaks like appointments — they are non-negotiable.
